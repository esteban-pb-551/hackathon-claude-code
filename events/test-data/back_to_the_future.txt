Back to the Future is a 1985 American science fiction film directed by Robert Zemeckis. The story follows Marty McFly, a teenager who is accidentally sent back in time from 1985 to 1955 using a time machine built from a DeLorean car by eccentric scientist Doc Brown. While in 1955, Marty inadvertently interferes with his parents' first meeting, threatening his own existence. He must find a way to reunite his parents and return to 1985.

The film explores themes of time travel, fate versus free will, and the impact of individual actions on the future. It features iconic scenes including the clock tower lightning strike sequence, the skateboard chase through Hill Valley, and the unforgettable moment when the DeLorean reaches 88 miles per hour.

Back to the Future was both a critical and commercial success, becoming the highest-grossing film of 1985. It spawned two sequels, an animated television series, and a theme park ride. The film is widely regarded as one of the greatest science fiction films ever made and has had a lasting impact on popular culture.
